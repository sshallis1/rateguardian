## Market Watch

**5 signals worth watching this week:**

1. **FOMC Decision (Wednesday 2pm ET)** — No rate change expected, but Powell's language matters. Any shift from "patient" to "concerned about growth" signals cuts are closer. This is Powell's penultimate meeting as Fed chair — he may tip his hand.

2. **Mag 7 Earnings (Tue-Thu)** — Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft, and Apple all report. If tech earnings disappoint, money rotates into defensives and rate-sensitive sectors. If they beat, risk-on continues and yields stay elevated.

3. **Housing Data Dump (Tue-Wed)** — Case-Shiller Home Price Index, FHFA Housing Price Index, Housing Starts, Building Permits, and New Home Sales all drop in 48 hours. This is the most housing data in a single week all quarter. Watch for cooling signals.

4. **10-Year at 4.31% on Iran Tensions** — Five straight sessions of rising yields driven by Strait of Hormuz disruptions, not domestic data. Geopolitical premiums unwind fast. If tensions ease, the 10-year drops and mortgage rates follow.

5. **VIX at 18.71** — Elevated but not panicking. The market is nervous, not fearful. A VIX above 20 on any Fed surprise would be the signal to watch rate-sensitive positions closely.

**Sectors in focus**: Housing, regional banks, big tech, bonds, energy
